# Implementation Intentions and Goal Achievement

## Overview

Implementation intentions are a psychological strategy for bridging the gap between intending to do something and actually doing it. The research is robust, with large meta-analyses showing significant effects.

## The Intention-Behavior Gap

**The problem:** There is a substantial gap between people's goal intentions and their goal achievement.

**Why:** Forming a goal intention alone does not prepare people sufficiently for dealing with self-regulatory problems.

**The evidence:** Meta-analysis revealed that a medium-to-large change in commitment (d = 0.66) only led to a small-to-medium change in behavior (d = 0.33). Strong intentions aren't enough.

## What Are Implementation Intentions?

Introduced by psychologist **Peter Gollwitzer** in 1999, implementation intentions are specific plans that spell out:

- **When** you will act
- **Where** you will act
- **How** you will act

**Format:** "If [situation X occurs], then I will [perform behavior Y]"

**Example:** Instead of "I will exercise more," specify "If it is 7am on Monday/Wednesday/Friday, then I will go to the gym before work."

## Meta-Analytic Evidence

### Gollwitzer & Sheeran (2006)

The landmark meta-analysis examining implementation intentions:

- **94 independent tests**
- **Effect size: d = 0.65** (medium-to-large)
- Implementation intentions were effective for:
  - Initiating goal striving
  - Shielding ongoing goal pursuit from distractions
  - Disengaging from failing courses of action
  - Conserving capability for future goal striving

### Recent Meta-Analysis (2024)

Updated analysis with **642 independent tests**:

- Effective for cognitive, affective, AND behavioral outcomes
- Effect sizes: 0.27 ≤ d ≤ 0.66
- Works across different age groups (children, adolescents, adults)
- Effects persist at long-term follow-ups

**Factors that increase effectiveness:**

- If-then contingent format
- High motivation toward the goal
- Rehearsing the plan at least once

## How Implementation Intentions Work

### "Passing Control to the Environment"

Gollwitzer describes implementation intentions as "passing the control of one's behavior on to the environment."

**Mechanism:**

1. Form an if-then plan
2. The specified situation becomes highly accessible in memory
3. When the situation occurs, behavior is triggered automatically
4. Removes hesitation and deliberation
5. Frees cognitive resources for other tasks

This is called **strategic automaticity** - deliberately creating automatic responses.

### The Role of Commitment

The strength of commitment to both the plan and the goal is crucial.

Without commitment, implementation intentions have little effect. Research shows that **plans made as commitments to another person** are particularly effective because breaking commitments generates discomfort.

## Mental Contrasting with Implementation Intentions (MCII)

A powerful combination:

1. **Mental contrasting:** After imagining a positive future, think about obstacles that impede the goal
2. **Implementation intentions:** Create if-then plans to address those obstacles

This transforms positive fantasies into binding goals by connecting them to reality.

## Practical Applications

Research shows that facilitating plan creation helps because it:

- Forces consideration of logistic obstacles
- Develops specific tactics to navigate around them
- Reduces underestimation of time required
- Helps people remember to act

## Relevance to Our Project

### How to Apply

An AI companion could help users:

1. **Declare specific tasks** (not vague goals)
2. **Specify when and where** they'll work on them
3. **Identify potential obstacles** during session start
4. **Create if-then plans** for handling distractions
5. **Rehearse the plan** by stating it aloud

### Example Session Start Flow

```
Companion: "What are you working on today?"
User: "The auth refactor"

Companion: "When specifically will you start on that?"
User: "Right now, for the next 45 minutes"

Companion: "What's likely to distract you?"
User: "Slack notifications"

Companion: "What's your plan if Slack pulls your attention?"
User: "If I notice I'm checking Slack, then I'll close it and return to the code"

Companion: "Great. You're working on the auth refactor for 45 minutes.
If Slack distracts you, you'll close it and return. Ready?"
```

### Why This Works for ADHD

- **Offloads working memory** - External system holds the plan
- **Creates external commitment** - Told the companion the plan
- **Pre-decides responses** - Don't need to deliberate when distracted
- **Specific over vague** - Concrete details are easier to act on
