Backed by Science

Body doubling isn't just a life hack - it's grounded in decades of psychological research on social facilitation, accountability, and executive function.

Social Facilitation Theory

Research by Robert Zajonc showed that the mere presence of others improves performance on well-learned tasks. Even a virtual presence can trigger this effect.

Implementation Intentions

Studies show that stating when, where, and how you'll do something dramatically increases follow-through. Our task declaration process builds this in naturally.

External Structure for ADHD

Research confirms that external accountability and structure are especially effective for those with ADHD, compensating for executive function challenges.
